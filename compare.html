No. 005 — Head-to-head

ChatGPT vs Claude vs Gemini: which should you actually use?

These three get compared constantly, and most comparisons either turn into a spec-sheet dump or a fan argument. Here's the honest version: what each one is actually built around, where they genuinely differ, and a simple way to decide without needing to trial all three for a month.

AssistantMade byKnown forNotable strength
ChatGPT OpenAI The most widely adopted general assistant, with a large ecosystem of plugins and custom GPTs Broad ecosystem and integrations — often the first place new AI features appear
Claude Anthropic Long-document handling and careful, well-structured writing and coding Strong at working through long files or codebases in one sitting
Gemini Google Deep integration across Search, Workspace, and Android Native multimodal input and tight integration if you already live in Google's tools

All three companies ship updates frequently, so treat this as a general shape rather than a permanent scoreboard — check each product's own site for its current capabilities before deciding.

For writing & drafting

All three can draft an email, an outline, or a first pass at an article competently. The differences show up more in editing behavior than in first drafts: some models lean toward heavier rewriting when asked to "improve" a passage, others stay closer to your original wording. If you have a distinct voice you want preserved, test the same paragraph in each and see which one edits with the lightest touch.

For coding

All three can explain, write, and debug code, and the practical difference for most people comes down to how each is integrated into your actual workflow — inside an editor, a terminal, or a plain chat window — rather than which model is "smarter" in the abstract. If you're coding daily, the tool's IDE integration matters more than which chatbot wins a benchmark.

For research & fact-finding

This is where it's worth being the most careful with all three: general-purpose assistants can state things confidently that are wrong, especially on fast-moving or very specific topics. For anything you're going to act on, cross-check with a live search or a primary source rather than trusting a single confident-sounding answer — a dedicated answer engine that shows its sources, like the one listed in the research family of the directory, is often a safer first stop for fact-finding than a general chatbot.

For everyday use

For quick questions, brainstorming, or working through a decision out loud, the differences between all three shrink a lot — this is the category where personal preference in tone and interface probably matters more than capability.

A simple way to decide

  • Already deep in Google Docs/Sheets/Gmail day to day? Gemini's integration will save you the most friction.
  • Working with long documents, contracts, or large codebases in one sitting? Claude tends to handle that kind of sustained, single-session work well.
  • Want the widest plugin/app ecosystem and the most third-party tools built around it? ChatGPT has the largest surrounding ecosystem right now.
  • Not sure? All three have usable free tiers — see the free tools guide and spend twenty minutes running the same real task through each before picking.

Frequently asked

Which AI assistant is best for coding?

All three can write and explain code competently. The practical difference usually comes down to editor/terminal integration rather than raw ability — try the free tier of each on a real task from your own work before committing to one.

Is Claude better than ChatGPT?

Neither is universally better. They trade off differently by task, and both change often enough that any fixed ranking goes stale within months. Testing both on your own real task beats trusting a general verdict.

Do I have to pick just one?

No — all three currently offer usable free tiers, and plenty of people keep more than one open and use whichever handles a given task better.
