No. 006 — Zero budget

Free AI tools you can actually use

"Free" in AI tools usually means one of three things: a genuinely useful free plan, a time-limited trial that quietly becomes a paid subscription, or a free tier so limited it's really just a demo. Here's which is which, for tools already in the directory.

Before you rely on any of these

Free tiers change often — usage caps, feature limits, and eligibility get adjusted without much notice. Treat everything below as a starting point and check the tool's own pricing page before you build a workflow around it.

Genuinely usable free plans

These offer real, ongoing free access — not just a trial.

Free tier

ChatGPT

Free access to a capable model

OpenAI offers a no-cost tier of ChatGPT with usage limits, alongside paid plans for higher usage and more capable models.

chatgpt.com

Free tier

Claude

Free access to a capable model

Anthropic offers Claude with a free usage allowance before prompting you toward a paid plan for higher limits.

claude.ai

Free tier

Gemini

Free access, tied to a Google account

Available free with any Google account, with usage limits and paid tiers for higher caps and more advanced features.

gemini.google.com

Free tier

Perplexity

Free search-style queries

Usable for free with daily limits on its more advanced search modes, which is normally enough for casual research use.

perplexity.ai

Free tier

Grammarly

Free grammar & clarity checks

The core grammar and spelling checker is free; deeper tone and style suggestions sit behind a paid plan.

grammarly.com

Free tier

Canva Magic Studio

Free design tools, some AI features gated

Canva's core editor is free to use; several Magic Studio AI features are limited or credit-based on the free plan.

canva.com/magic-studio

Free, but with real strings attached

Worth trying, but check the fine print before you depend on them.

Limited free

Otter.ai

Free transcription with monthly caps

Includes a free tier with a monthly transcription-minutes limit — fine for occasional use, tight for anyone recording meetings daily.

otter.ai

Trial-based

Jasper & Copy.ai

Free trial, then paid

Both are built around paid subscriptions for teams doing marketing copy at volume, with a limited free trial rather than an ongoing free plan.

jasper.ai

Conditional free

GitHub Copilot

Free for eligible students & open source

Historically free for verified students and some open-source maintainers, with a paid subscription for everyone else — eligibility rules are worth checking directly on GitHub's site since they do change.

github.com/features/copilot
